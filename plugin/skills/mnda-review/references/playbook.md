# MNDA Legal Playbook

12 clause checks. For each: keywords to look for, red-flag terms, the standard position, and exact preferred replacement language for redlines.

---

## 1. Mutual Structure
**Severity if missing:** RED
**Keywords:** mutual, both parties, each party, reciprocal
**Red-flag terms:** one-way, unilateral, disclosing party only

**Standard position:** The NDA must impose confidentiality obligations on both parties equally.

**Preferred language:**
> Each Party agrees to hold the other Party's Confidential Information in strict confidence and shall not disclose it to any third party without prior written consent.

**Redline:** Change asymmetric Disclosing/Receiving Party structure to mutual obligations on both parties.

---

## 2. Survival Period
**Severity if missing:** YELLOW
**Keywords:** survive, survival, termination, years, expiration
**Red-flag terms:** perpetual, indefinitely, no expiration

**Standard position:** Preferred survival period is 3-5 years post-termination. Perpetual = RED. Under 2 years = YELLOW.

**Preferred language:**
> The obligations of confidentiality shall survive termination for a period of five (5) years.

---

## 3. Governing Law
**Severity if missing:** YELLOW
**Keywords:** governing law, jurisdiction, governed by, laws of
**Red-flag terms:** mandatory arbitration, foreign jurisdiction

**Standard position:** Delaware or California preferred. Other US states = YELLOW. Non-US = RED.

**Preferred language:**
> This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to conflict of law principles.

---

## 4. Injunctive Relief
**Severity if missing:** YELLOW
**Keywords:** injunctive relief, equitable relief, irreparable harm, specific performance
**Red-flag terms:** (flag if absent)

**Standard position:** Must include injunctive/equitable relief clause without bond requirement.

**Preferred language:**
> Each Party acknowledges that breach may cause irreparable harm and the non-breaching Party shall be entitled to seek equitable relief without posting any bond or security.

---

## 5. Standard of Care
**Severity if missing:** YELLOW
**Keywords:** reasonable care, same degree of care, protect, safeguard
**Red-flag terms:** absolute, strict liability, guarantee confidentiality

**Standard position:** "Reasonable care" or "same degree of care as own information." Absolute obligations = unacceptable.

**Preferred language:**
> Each Party shall protect the other's Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care as its own, but no less than reasonable care.

---

## 6. Exceptions to Confidentiality
**Severity if missing:** RED
**Keywords:** public domain, publicly available, prior knowledge, independently developed, legally required, court order
**Red-flag terms:** (flag if any of the four exceptions are absent)

**Standard position:** All four carve-outs required: (a) publicly available, (b) prior knowledge, (c) independently developed, (d) legally compelled.

**Preferred language:**
> Confidential Information excludes information that: (a) is publicly available through no fault of Receiving Party; (b) was known prior to disclosure; (c) is independently developed; or (d) is legally required to be disclosed with prior written notice.

---

## 7. Return or Destruction
**Severity if missing:** YELLOW
**Keywords:** return, destroy, destruction, deletion, delete, upon request, upon termination
**Red-flag terms:** (flag if absent)

**Standard position:** Obligation to return or destroy on request or termination, with written certification.

**Preferred language:**
> Upon request or termination, each Party shall return or destroy all Confidential Information and certify such in writing within ten (10) business days.

---

## 8. No-Solicitation Clause
**Severity if present:** YELLOW
**Keywords:** solicit, solicitation, recruit, hire, employ
**Red-flag terms:** non-solicit, non-recruitment, no solicitation of employees

**Standard position:** Exclude no-solicitation clauses from NDAs. If present, propose deletion.

**Redline:** Delete the no-solicitation clause entirely.

---

## 9. Unilateral NDA Detection
**Severity if detected:** RED
**Keywords:** disclosing party, receiving party
**Red-flag terms:** only the receiving party, solely the receiving party

**Standard position:** One-way NDA must be returned for revision.

**Preferred language:**
> Each Party may act as both disclosing and receiving party, and obligations apply equally to both.

---

## 10. Definition of Confidential Information
**Severity if overbroad:** YELLOW
**Keywords:** confidential information means, proprietary information, includes without limitation
**Red-flag terms:** all information shared, any and all information

**Standard position:** Clear, scoped definition. Overly broad = flag for narrowing.

**Preferred language:**
> "Confidential Information" means any non-public information designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature and circumstances of disclosure.

---

## 11. Agreement Term / Duration
**Severity if perpetual:** YELLOW
**Keywords:** term, duration, effective date, expires, years
**Red-flag terms:** perpetual, indefinite term, no end date

**Standard position:** 1-3 year term with 30 days notice for early termination.

**Preferred language:**
> This Agreement shall remain in effect for two (2) years from the Effective Date, unless earlier terminated by either Party upon thirty (30) days written notice.

---

## 12. Permitted Disclosures (Need to Know)
**Severity if missing:** YELLOW
**Keywords:** need to know, employees, contractors, advisors, affiliates
**Red-flag terms:** unlimited disclosure, may share with anyone

**Standard position:** Limit to employees, contractors, advisors with genuine need to know, bound by equivalent obligations.

**Preferred language:**
> Each Party may disclose only to employees, contractors, and advisors who (a) need to know for the Purpose and (b) are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as this Agreement.
